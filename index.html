Research Framework

How I think about a commodity market.

The objective isn't to predict every market move. It is to understand the physical fundamentals, quantify the risks and identify where the market may be underestimating an important variable.

01

Start with the physical world

Crop conditions, inventories, logistics, infrastructure, weather and physical trade flows.

02

Build the balance

Translate observations into supply, demand, production, consumption and stock estimates.

03

Identify the catalyst

Weather, policy, geopolitics, freight, regulation or infrastructure disruption.

04

Quantify the scenario

Use forecasting, regression, Monte Carlo simulation, correlation and sensitivity analysis.

05

Ask what isn't priced in

Look for anomalies, structural breaks, hidden risks and assumptions that may be missing from consensus.

Green Line Commodities · Renewable Fuels
2026

Identifying an anomaly before regulatory scrutiny intensified

How can physical supply constraints reveal anomalies in renewable fuel markets before they become obvious in prices?

Built a quarterly supply-demand framework for Europe's HVO market and Germany's THG certificate market, combining paid pricing data with public regulatory registries.

Developed a physical plausibility check comparing reported used cooking oil imports against an estimated ceiling for available waste-oil supply in Europe.

The framework also incorporated price decomposition across HVO, gasoil and THG certificates to separate feedstock-driven effects from the green premium.

Independent Research · Fertilizer
Scenario Analysis

What happens if the Strait of Hormuz closes for 40 days?

How would a prolonged Hormuz disruption affect a Pakistani urea trading book?

Modeled three simultaneous shocks: gas curtailment affecting domestic urea production, shipping rerouting around the Cape of Good Hope, and crop-yield sensitivity to nutrient shortages across Sindh and Punjab.

Built an inventory timeline to estimate when domestic buffer stocks could become constrained, then ran Monte Carlo simulations across randomized blockade durations and price paths.

Key insight A simple long-urea, short-wheat hedge was insufficient to protect trading margin through a full closure scenario.

Louis Dreyfus Company · Cotton Research
July 2025

The forecast I was honest about

Can seasonal time-series behavior help anticipate where cotton basis may move next?

Applied a seasonal ARIMA framework to nine years of cotton basis data to investigate recurring seasonal behavior and potential mean-reversion patterns.

The model identified a seasonal pattern in which elevated basis levels in a given month tended to reverse during the corresponding month of the following year.

Rather than presenting the forecast as certainty, confidence intervals were explicitly communicated and the need for additional research was highlighted before extending the model further.

Louis Dreyfus Company · Punjab Cotton Desk
September 2025

Measuring a flood before the balance sheet needed a number

How do you estimate crop damage when the season is still unfolding?

Helped run a 284-sample yield survey across Bahawalpur, Multan and Dera Ghazi Khan during the Punjab cotton season.

Following major rainfall, mapped flood exposure at the tehsil level, focusing on Alipur and Jalalpur Peer Wala rather than assuming uniform impact across entire districts.

Cross-checked farmer-reported yields against public harvest pace data and incorporated historical cotton arrivals to distinguish genuine supply disruption from normal seasonal variation.

Key insight Combining field observations, flood extent, crop exposure, harvest pace and historical arrivals produced a more decision-relevant estimate than relying on a single survey source.
